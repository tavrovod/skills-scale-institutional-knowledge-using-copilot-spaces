# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Test Engineers

### Role Summary
QA/Test Engineers develop and execute testing strategies, define quality standards, and ensure software meets functional and non-functional requirements before release.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test strategies and test plans
- Write automated and manual test cases
- Execute regression testing and quality assurance
- Identify and document defects with clear reproduction steps
- Collaborate with developers on testing approaches
- Track quality metrics and test coverage

### Goals
- Ensure software meets quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Minimize production defects and regressions
- Reduce cycle time for testing and validation
- Maintain high customer confidence in releases

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and test strategy discussions
- Bug reports and test coverage metrics
- Release readiness sign-offs

---

## DevOps/Release Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps/Release Engineers manage infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and release processes. They enable safe, reliable, and frequent software deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure and cloud resources
- Plan and execute deployments and releases
- Monitor system health and performance
- Coordinate with developers on deployment requirements
- Manage release notes and deployment documentation

### Goals
- Enable safe, fast, and frequent deployments
- Maintain system reliability and uptime
- Reduce deployment time and failure rates
- Ensure clear communication about release contents

### Typical Communication
- Release coordination meetings and deployment schedules
- Infrastructure planning and technical design reviews
- Deployment status and incident communications

---

## Technical Architects

### Role Summary
Technical Architects provide technical direction, design oversight, and ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with long-term technical strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical vision and architectural principles
- Review and guide system design decisions
- Ensure technology choices support project and business goals
- Mentor developers on design patterns and best practices
- Document architectural decisions and trade-offs
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Ensure technically sound and scalable solutions
- Reduce technical debt and refactoring costs
- Enable team growth and knowledge transfer
- Align technical decisions with business strategy

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Decision logs and design documentation
- Technical mentoring and design pattern guidance

---

## Stakeholder/Executive Sponsors

### Role Summary
Stakeholder/Executive Sponsors represent business interests, provide governance oversight, and ensure projects align with organizational strategy and priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success criteria
- Approve project scope, budget, and schedule
- Provide governance oversight and escalation resolution
- Communicate project status to leadership
- Represent organizational interests and constraints
- Approve go/no-go decisions for major releases

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers business value
- Maintain alignment with organizational strategy
- Manage risks and escalations at executive level
- Ensure stakeholder confidence and communication

### Typical Communication
- Executive steering committee meetings
- Status reports and business case reviews
- Escalation and decision discussions

---

## Scrum Master/Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters/Agile Coaches facilitate agile processes, remove team impediments, and coach teams on continuous improvement practices.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, standup, review, retro)
- Coach team on agile practices and ceremonies
- Remove impediments and escalate blockers
- Track team velocity and process metrics
- Facilitate continuous improvement discussions
- Protect team focus and resolve distractions

### Goals
- Enable predictable, sustainable team delivery
- Foster team collaboration and continuous improvement
- Reduce process friction and cycle time
- Support team development and self-organization

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremonies and process facilitation
- Retrospective insights and improvement tracking
- Impediment resolution and team coaching
